Routine Addendums (24 hour TAT)

Once the update has hit TAT in needs addendum status (24 hour mark):

  • Confirm the study comments are updated properly.
  • Confirm the exam is assigned to the original reading radiologist.
  • Was the report released or is it a preliminary report?

If released:

The exam should remain in the reading Radiologist work list. Follow up and include that the update has been pending for 24 hours now.

If preliminary report:

Confirm that the original reading Radiologist was reached out to. If no response or update, remove the report (add to the drive) and reassign for a complete new read.

Once the update has hit 48 hours in needs addendum status: ESCALATE

Please be sure to NOT call the doctors without permission from Operations or a Lead. The ONLY time the team should immediately call a doctor without approval is for a STROKE exam. Outside of receiving a stroke, it should be escalated before calling a doctor.

When communicating with the doctors, the first option should be via text/Google chat. The Radiologist information has their preferred way to communicate which will provide you with the number to text or direct you to Google chat.

Escalation Procedures

High Level Escalations

  • IT Issues in which a Facility/Hospital cannot send exams. Escalate to Management
  • Radiologist out for an emergency with a large gap in coverage. After all avenues have been exhausted in reaching additional Radiologists. Escalate to Management
  • Ramsoft or Emsow PACS is down
  • Ramsoft or Emsow services are inoperative or highly degraded. Escalate to Management for short-term workaround
  • Multiple Radiologists unable to dictate. Escalate to Management
  • If there are no Credentialed Radiologists to read for the Hospital location (e.g., Early Medical Hospital, Weekend with very few credentialed doctors). Escalate to the Operations team
  • If there is a critical finding and the team is not able to reach anyone from the Imaging centers or the Hospital. Escalate to the Operations team

Medium Level Escalations

  • Code Stroke - Anything nearing 30 mins. Must be escalated to Operations
  • STAT Exams - Anything approaching over 1 hour follows normal escalation protocol. Anything over 2 hours must be escalated to Operations
  • Single Radiologist unable to dictate. Escalate to Operations
  • Facility/Hospital HL7 down. Follow normal process to fax or email reports. Repush reports via HL7 once HL7 is back up. Escalate to Operations for their awareness

Low Level Escalations

  • Specialty exams should follow the normal rule of escalation. Any specialty exams reaching over 2 hours (e.g., OB over received as a STAT). Should be escalated to Operations
  • Problems that affect non-essential functions and have minimal impact on system or user performance. Should be escalated to Operations and/or IT via email request
  • Software configuration/system enhancement requests. Should be escalated to Operations and/or IT via email request
  • Radiologist not having enough routine exams and asking for more can be escalated next morning. They can send an email on the same day so that Operations team can have a look into it.
